jordan peterson 35 tips to develop a strong work ethic

1. Show up on time.
2. Aim for promotion on the basis of your productivity and utility.
3. Be generous with sharing credit.
4. Make sure your goals at work line up with your goals in life.
5. Don't be afraid to be your own advocate.
6. Listen carefully and tell the truth.
7. Eat a protein and fat rich breakfast every day.
8. Don't sacrifice medium and long-term progress to expediency.
9. Get up early.
10. Do a couple of unpleasant but necessary tasks every day.
11. Read as much as you can.
12. Take your vacations.
13. Do the things you say will do.
14. Take your coworkers to lunch.
15. Don't sacrifice yourself to the point of resentment.
16. Learn to say "no" when it is necessary.
17. Work hard on expanding and maintaining your social networks.
18. Put yourself in a position of strength before you negotiate.
19. Do not participate in office gossip.
20. Delegate enough to develop your juniors.
21. Learn to speak publicly.
22. Write clearly, carefully and concisely.
23. Buy clothes that are slightly better than you can afford.
24. Learn that responsibility and opportunity are synonymous.
25. Have an updated CV on hand at all times.
26. If you have to switch companies to advance your career, don't be stopped by fear.
27. Fire people who demotivate others.
28. Let your juniors know when they do something particularly well.
29. Take some time off when your opportunities start to feel like obligations.
30. Have a home life.
31. Learn the difference between real work and pseudo-work.
32. Protect your time.
33. Surround yourself with straightforward, productive people.
34. Ask a question if you don't know something.
35. Object to stupid new rules.
