Onmyouji by YUMEMAKURA Baku
Translated by Mink
Chapter One
___I am about to tell the story of an extraordinary man.
___He was such a man, that if one were to compare him to something, it would be to a cloud adrift on the wind, floating in the emptiness of the night. 
___Clouds that float in the darkness cannot be seen to have changed their shape at all from one instant to the next, but if you watch them, their form changes without your realizing it. Even though you believe it to be the same cloud, there is nothing in its form for one to catch onto. 
___This is the story of such a man. 
___His name was Abeno Seimei*.
___He was an Onmyouji*.
___He said to have been born around the twenty-first year of the Engi Period*, during the reign of Emperor Daigo, but the year of this character’s death has no direct relation to this story. It may better suit the nature of this tale if such numbers are left unclear. 
___I cannot tell at present.
___I will, I think, let my brush continue freely, and respond to things as best I can as they happen. That style is most likely more suited to the telling of this man’s story. 
___The Heian period*-- 
___It was a time when darkness still remained as darkness, and a good part of mankind was still confident in their belief in the existence of the supernatural. This was not only in the far-off forests and mountains, but in this period, people, and oni*, and mononoke* were all in the darkness of the capitol, and at times under the same roof, drawing breaths and living together. 
___Onmyouji—
___To put it simply, an onmyouji could be called a master of fortune-telling. One could also call it an enchanter or shaman, but none of these gives a precise definition. 
___An onmyouji reads the phases of the stars, and reads the phases of men.
___He reads these points, tells fortunes, depending on the spell he uses, he can kill a man with a curse, and he also performs illusions. 
___Powers which cannot be seen by the eye—fate, spirits, oni; he has a deep connection with these kinds of mysterious beings, and has the skills to control them. 
___It was one of the positions of the imperial court, and there was even a chamber prepared for the use of the ___Onmyouji in the innermost part of the palace grounds. 
___Seimei himself was awarded the rank of fourth below the Emperor in the court.
___First was the dajodaijin, the grand minister of state.
___Second was naidaijin, the minister of state.
___Third was the chief councilor of state and the councilor of state.
___With his work he had quite a bit of influence, even in the court. 
___In Tales of Past and Present, there are written many interesting tales about this Abeno Seimei.
___According to those, from his youth, Seimei trained under an onmyouji called Kamono Tadayuki.
___From that time, Seimei showed a spark of the particular abilities of an onmyouji.
___It seems he was a genius of sorts.
___There is a story in Tales of Past and Present in which his teacher, Tadayuki, goes downtown* one evening while Seimei was still young. 
___Downtown meaning the southern parts.
___He left the palace grounds through the Suzaku gate and may have taken Suzaku Boulevard all the way down to near Rajou Gate on the outskirts of the south of the capitol. 
___From the center of the palace grounds to Rajou Gate is roughly eight ri*. 
___He went by cart.
___It is not written as to what kind of cart this was, but it may have been an ox cart. 
___Why he would go downtown at night is, of course, not written, but he may have been stealing away to visit a female acquaintance of his. This kind of situation seems appropriate.
___Semei was also in the midst of this procession.
___Tadayuki alone was in the cart, and the rest were on foot. 
___This procession was, including Seimei, two or three people at most. One to lead the ox, another to hold a lamp—and the third was young Seimei. His age is not mentioned, but at this time, I would imagine his age to be just barely into his early teens. 
___The others in the procession wore sensible hitatare,* but it is said that Seimei wore a somewhat old hakama* and was barefoot. What he wore was probably somebody’s old clothes.
___Even when dressed in used clothing, in the intelligence in which he wrapped himself, and in his features, there could be seen dignity and eloquence, or so it is surely believed, but this was not true. While his features were noble, the rest of his appearance was no different from any other boy of the age before their hair was tied up. 
___On some occasions, he would display frightfully adult and mysterious speech and behaviors. He was that kind of child. 
___His teacher, Tadayuki may have seen, on occasion, in the young Seimei’s eyes, a flicker of mysterious intelligence that was not found in others. But probably, that was all. 
___It was the events of that night in which Tadayuki first got close to the talents hidden within Seimei. 
___Let us return to the story.
___The ox car continued on, and approached the southern outskirts of the capitol.
___Tadayuki was fast asleep inside the cart.
___Walking alongside the cart, Seimei happened to look behind them and saw a strange sight.
___"Indescribably fearsome oni"* were walking towards the cart from behind. 
___When the other members of the procession looked, it was as if they could not see the oni at all.
___Seimei opened the cart window at once and said,
___"Master Tadayuki!"
___He woke the sleeping Tadayuki and told of the things he had seen.
___The now-awake Tadayuki stuck his head out from the cart and looked back to see the oni who had, sure enough, gotten closer to the cart. 
___"Stop the cart," Tadayuki called out to the members of the procession.
___"Hide in the shadow of the ox cart, hide your breathing and don’t move. Above all, you must not make a sound."
___Tadayuki used his illusions and made the ox cart and them invisible to the eyes of the oni, and let them pass. It was after this night that Tadayuki came to have Seimei at his side at all times. 
___Tadayuki, as you know, taught Seimei everything of the path of Yin and Yang. 
___"Like reproducing water in a vessel"
___Such a story is in Tales of Past and Present.
___The meaning of this is taking the water that is in the vessel called Kamono Tadayuki, that is to say the knowledge of Yin and Yang, and reproducing it exactly in the vessel called Abeno Seimei. 
___After the death of Tadayuki, it is said that Seimei’s residence was north of Tsuchimikado* road and east of Nishinotouin* road. 
___Looking from the Shishiden* in the center of the palace, it was to the north east, that is to say, it was in the direction between the ox and tiger on the wheel of the twelve sacred animals.* 
___The direction of the ox and tiger, namely, the oni’s gate.*
___That to the northeast of the Heian capitol was the Enryaku Temple on Mount Hiei, and to the northeast of the palace were the quarters of Abeno Seimei, the Onmyouji, in a kind of double construction, was of course, no accident. 
___This system of layout of the Heian capitol was in order to protect Emperor Kanmu from the vengeful spirit of his sibling Sawara, who had become next in line to the throne after the assiassination of Fujiwarano Tanetsugu. It was for this purpose that he abandoned the capitol at Nagaoka after ten years and began constructing the Heian capitol. 
___However, this was before Seimei’s birth. It has no direct relation to this story. 
___Feel free to read the story in Tales of Past and Present as well. 
___Continuing on.
___In that residence in the direction of the Oni’s gate, lived Seimei, and one day a lone magician came to visit him. He brought with him two attendants of about ten years of age. 
___"For what reason have you come?" Seimei asked. 
___"I am one who lives in the land of Harima*," he replied. 
___His name was Chitoku. 
___After telling his name, the magician began to tell his tale
___I have been thinking for a while that I would like to learn the ways of yin and yang. From what I have heard, the one who is most gifted in that are is you. Even a little is enough, but would you be unwilling to teach me?
___The magician called Chitoku said something like this to Seimei.
___"What?" Seimei thought at hearing the words of this magician. 
___"This magician must be a leaned man of this path. He has come to test me."
___Seimei recognized the true identity of the magician, thinking there is no doubt that this man surpasses all in the way of the path on yin and yang, and he has come to test me. 
___It would be that the two attendants that accompanied him were shikijin.* 
___"Shikijin" could be written with the characters "discriminating god" or "ceremony god." 
___They were also called ‘Shikishin’ or ‘Shikigami,’ and in one school of thought of the way of yin and yang that still remains today, the Izanagi school in Shikoku, they are called by the name of ‘Shikiouji*.’
___They are spirits that would not normally be visible to the eye. 
___They are not particularly advanced spirits. They are rough spirits. By spells they can be controlled as Shikijin, but depending on the ability of the onmyouji, they become advanced or elementary rough spirits. 
___"Oh-ho," he said nodding. This is a man of some skill.
___Seimei was impressed. 
___The shikijin that the magician who called himself Chitoku was using were not something that one would have been able to manage to use without polished skill. 
___"I have heard your request. However, today I have some responsibilities to which I must attend."
___Once you have returned home, could you choose a suitable date and come call on me again, Seimei said to the magician. 
___While saying this, he thrust both of his hands into his sleeves, and there secretly tied a seal and quietly chanted a spell.
___"Could you choose a suitable day and…"
___The magician held his hands out face up, touched his forehead to them and went home.
___However, Seimei did not move. 
___He stood there with his arms still crossed, looking up at the sky.
___Before long, when he thought the magician had gone one or two chou*, he saw the magician come back through the still-open gate. While walking, he was looking in the porch and everywhere it seemed a person could be hiding. 
___The magician stood once again before Seimei. 
___"The truth is, I’ve suddenly lost sight of the two attendants that should have been following after me. Would you please return them?" The magician said.
___"Return them?" Seimei said to the magician, feigning innocence. 
___"I have done nothing. You should know of the two boys who were with you. How could one such as I, who has done nothing but stand here, have hidden those attendants?"
___The magician, upon hearing this bowed his head to Seimei and said, 
___"I am sorry. They were actually not attendants, but the shikijin that I use. I came here to test the extent of your abilities, but that was not within the limits of mine. Please forgive me."
___He was trembling.
___"I don’t mind that you wanted to test me, but I cannot be fooled by such a half-hearted attempt." 
___Seimei suddenly changed his manner of speaking and smiled.
___The smile which appeared on his lips was, while one would not call it vulgar, was probably also not elegant. 
___Through those lips he muttered a spell. 
___When he did, the two attendants instantly came running outdoors. 
___Those two attendants were holding in their hands various kinds of fish and sake.
___"You were made to atone there right away. Since you received those to let you enjoy your time, take them home with you," Seimei said in a queer way. 
___Or so it would be interesting if he did. That much is not written in Tales of Past and Present.
___The two attendants just went running back. 
___The magician thought with great depth of feeling, 
___"Even so, it has been a simple thing to use shikijin since long ago, but to hide the shikijin of another, that is not something an ordinary man could do."
___He was excited and even his face turned red. 
___I would like very much for you to take me on as an apprentice, the magician said and wrote his name card and handed it to Seimei. 
___Writing one’s own name, and giving it to another was not something that was often done between those who worked magic. 
___It has the same degree of meaning as entrusting that person with one’s life. 
___The story in Tales of Past and Present continues.
___At another time.
___Seimei went to the residence of one called Bishop Hirosawano Kanchou. 
___Numerous young nobles and monks started talking to Seimei. 
___Everyone had heard rumors of Seimei, and naturally the topic of conversation was magic.
___"About using shikijin, for example, could one use them to kill another man?" One asked out of the blue.
___"How rudely you ask of the secrets of this path."
___With an intentionally fearsome gaze, he may have glared at the noble who asked that question.
___And after briefly enjoying the raw fear that appeared in that noble’s eyes replied, 
___"No, they are not something that can kill humans that easily."
___He would grin ever so slightly, and put the noble at ease, and then,
___"Well, there are various other ways, as well."
___He may have added something to that extent. 
___"If that is so, then would it be a simple matter to kill a small insect?" Another noble would ask.
___"Well, I suppose."
___When Seimei answered like that, five or six frogs were hopping about the garden.
___"Could you kill one of those?" A noble said.
___"Yes, but aside from killing…."
___"Is there something else?"
___"Even though one can kill, one cannot bring things back to life. The unnecessary taking of life is an offense."
___"Just one of those…"
___"I should like to see it as well."
___"As would I."
___"And I."
___And so the nobles and monks grouped together. 
___Rumors aside, those who had actually seen Seimei’s skills were only a few, after all, and their eyes all shone at once with curiosity. 
___If, at this point, Seimei refused to perform any of the tricks of which he had been speaking, leaving it at that, their eyes had a look that said it would become the basis of a rumor that "that man isn’t all they make him out to be."
___Seimei took a sharp look at their faces and said, "It is a terrible thing you make me do."
___He made a short utterance and extended his arms. 
___Offhandedly, he plucked with white fingers a new leaf that hung down from a willow branch. 
___Suddenly, he threw that leaf up into the air and chanted a spell.
___The willow leaf flew through the air and gently floated down on top of one of the frogs. Just at that moment, the frog was crushed flat and died. 
___Its flesh and innards must have sprayed around the area.
___"The nobles saw this, and paled in fear,*" tells the Tales of Past and Present. 
___When no people were present in his residence, it seems that Seimei used shikijin.
___Although no one was there, blinds would raise and lower and although there was no one do to so, the gate would open and close.
___It seemed these varied mysteries occurred around Seimei.
___To look at other sources, it seems that this Abeno Seimei, like the examples of the magician Chitoku and the frogs, used his skills recklessly to surprise people. 
___It seemed he enjoyed that sort of thing. Although he assumed airs with a satisfied look on his face, he had some child-like points as well.
___From here on the story is from my imagination, but I believe this man called Abeno Seimei, while working in the palace, also got involved in some fairly interesting situations on the side.
___He was tall, and pale, and must have been a graceful and beautiful man with a sad look to his eyes. 
___When he strolled about in a refined way, the women of the court would gaze at him and tell rumors amongst themselves. 
___He must have received one or two letters containing suggestive poems from women of royal lineage.
___He was tactful in his dealings with superiors, or so one would think, but he used a blunt way of speech. 
___It is possible he carelessly spoke out to the emperor saying, "Hey, you."
___His same lips that would show a refined smile would, at other times, hold a very vulgar one.
___The position of onmyouji required he transverse the underside of the way of men, and being in court required he have an excellent education.
___He had to have memorized a fair number of Chinese poems, had skill in music, and must have played biwa* or flute, a musical instrument or two fairly well. 
___I think of the Heian period as a time of refined darkness. 
___I intend to tell the story of a man who flowed, aloof, like a cloud carried by the wind through that graceful, refined and ghastly darkness.

Abeno Seimei: Abeno is his family name, and Seimei his personal name. 
Onmyouji: Literally, a Yin-Yang magician. 
Engi period: The period before Heian in Japanese history. It lasted from 901 to 923. The 21st year of Engi would be 922.
Heian period: It lasted from 923 and for about 400 years. It is generally split into three times: Early, Middle and Late. The Heian capitol is what is now Kyoto. 
Oni: Japanese demons. I hesitate to translate this as ‘demon’ because it conjures an image of something more evil and menacing than oni. Oni did do mean things and were violent, but in a different sense than the demons of Christianity. 
Mononoke: A spirit that would inhabit a person, animal or object. They were generally intent on causing harm and trouble. 
Downtown: (shimogyou): A name for the part of Kyoto south of Second Street (nijou)
Ri: An old Japanese measurement. It is approximately 4km.
Hitatare: Ceremonial court robes
Hakama: The skirt-like pants that samurai, etc. wear.
Indescribably Fearsome Oni: This is the name of a story in Tales of Past and Present.
Tsuchimikado: Mikado means "honorable gate" and this is the name of the upper eastern and western gates to the palace grounds of the Heian capitol. The Tsuchimikado road is the road that runs through them. It was on the north side of the city. 
Nishinotouin: A street that ran north-south through the center of Heian Kyoto.
Shishinden: "purple emperor’s palace." This was where official business was carried out. 
Twelve sacred animals: From the Chinese horoscopes. Each animal is associated with a certain direction, time, element, etc. 
Oni’s gate: According to principles of yin and yang, this is the direction from which evil comes, so oni are said to enter and leave the city from this direction. 
Harima: This is a province located near what is now Kobe. 
Shikijin: Spirits that follow the orders of an onmyouji and can take many forms and have many mysterious powers. More information can be found on this website.
Shijiouji: Written with the Chinese characters for "ceremony prince."
Chou: A measurement of distance. In the Heian capitol, it was about 400 feet. 
The nobles saw this, and paled in fear: The name of another story in Tales of Past and Present. 
Biwa: A four-stringed Japanese lute.
